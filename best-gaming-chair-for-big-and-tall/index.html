Frequently asked questions

What should big and tall buyers look for in a gaming chair?

Weight capacity is the first number to check: a chair rated for 250 pounds will wear out faster and fail sooner if you are near or above that limit. Look for at least 300 to 400 pounds capacity and confirm it is the actual structural limit, not a marketing claim. Seat width matters more than most reviews mention: a narrow 20-inch seat that pinches your hips is uncomfortable regardless of how good the lumbar support is. Backrest height needs to reach at least to your upper shoulders; for users over six feet, the standard 33-inch backrest may leave your upper back unsupported. The XL or regular size variant of a chair can make a significant difference, and both Secretlab and DXRacer offer explicit size variants you should verify before ordering.

What weight limit should I look for?

Always look for a weight limit at least 50 pounds above your actual weight to account for long-term wear on the gas cylinder and frame joints. Budget chairs rated at 250 pounds often use components that degrade faster under sustained use near that limit. Mid-range and premium chairs with ratings of 300 to 400 pounds typically use heavier-gauge steel and higher-quality gas cylinders that hold up better over years. The gas cylinder is the component most likely to fail first: if you exceed the rated limit or are consistently near it, budget for a cylinder replacement after a few years, or buy a chair with a higher limit in the first place.
