Frequently asked questions

Is the Secretlab Titan Evo worth the price?

The Secretlab Titan Evo is a well-built chair with a steel frame and dense foam that holds its shape for years. If you are over 5 feet 10 inches and want a sturdy seat that looks aggressive and lasts, it is a solid purchase. The built-in lumbar support is adjustable but not as flexible as a separate office chair mechanism; it works well for a narrow range of backs. For people who prefer a soft, plush seat or need extensive adjustability like seat depth or tilt lock angle, the Titan will feel too firm and restrictive. It is also heavy and the PU leather can be hot in warm rooms. If you value durability and consistent support and do not care about breathability or fine-tuning, the Titan is worth the premium.

How does the Secretlab Titan compare to cheaper gaming chairs?

The most obvious difference is material quality. Budget chairs use bonded leather that peels within a year; the Titan uses a proprietary SoftWeave fabric or high-grade PU leather that holds up significantly longer with daily use. The steel frame and aluminum base are meaningfully heavier and more stable than the frames you find in chairs half the price. The integrated lumbar adjustment via a dial on the right side eliminates the fussy loose lumbar pillow that budget chairs rely on. If you are comparing it to a $200 chair, the Titan is a clear upgrade. If you are comparing it to a $700 chair from Herman Miller, the ergonomic gap opens up and the office chair wins on adjustability.

What sizes does the Secretlab Titan come in?

Secretlab makes the Titan in Regular and XL. The Regular fits most users between 5 feet 7 inches and 6 feet 2 inches with a weight limit of around 285 pounds. The XL is for users between 5 feet 11 inches and 6 feet 9 inches with a weight limit of around 395 pounds. Pay attention to both the height range and the seat width, not just the weight limit: if you have a wider build, the XL provides more seat width even if your height falls in the Regular range. Secretlab has a sizing tool on their website that asks for height and weight; use it before ordering because returns are available but the chair is heavy and awkward to repackage.
